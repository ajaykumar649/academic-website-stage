Hi, I am **Dr. Ajay Kumar**, an Assistant Professor at the Department of Physics, Institute of Science, Banaras Hindu University (BHU). 

I was born and grew up in the small town of Nawada, located within the ancient Magadh division of south Bihar. Following my schooling at Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalaya, Rewar, Nawada, I moved to Delhi to pursue higher academic paths. 

I joined the faculty at BHU in July 2020 after spending several years conducting physics instrumentation frameworks in the United States as a Post-Doctoral Fellow, Pre-Doctoral Fellow, Research Intern, and Scholar across **Purdue University**, **Northwestern University**, and **Fermilab**. Prior to arriving at BHU, I served as an Assistant Professor at Sri Aurobindo College (M), University of Delhi.

I received my Ph.D. from the **University of Delhi** in 2016. My doctoral defense thesis focused on: *“Search for the SM Higgs boson in the H to WW decay channel and Probe of WW Production in Vector Boson Fusion Topology in the CMS Experiment at the LHC”*. My core graduate research foundations were spent handling on-site data pipelines across **CERN**, **Fermilab**, and the **University of Delhi**.

---

### 🔬 Core Research Vectors

* **Detector Instrumentation & R&D:** Simulation, structural configuration optimization, and electrical performance modeling of gas-based **GEM detectors** (using ANSYS and Garfield++) and **Low-Gain Avalanche Detectors (LGADs)** for radiation-hard CMS tracking upgrades.
* **Electroweak & Higgs Physics:** Searching for Standard Model Higgs decay properties, measuring Electroweak vector boson scattering (VBS), and extracting strict boundary limits on anomalous Gauge Couplings.
* **Nucleon Structure & Heavy Ions:** Investigating Generalized Parton Distributions via Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering (DVCS) simulation pipelines for the ATHENA experiment at EIC, as well as heavy hadron production dynamics inside hot Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) frameworks using PHENIX data.
